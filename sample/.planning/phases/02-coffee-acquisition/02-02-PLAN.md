# Plan 02-02: Cup Handling and Fill-Level Detection

## Goal

The robot places a cup under the spout, detects when it is full, and removes
it without spilling. This is the longest document in the sample — use it to
test scrolling.

## Background

Cup handling has three hard sub-problems:

1. **Grip strength** — paper cups deform above 4 N, ceramic slips below 8 N.
2. **Placement tolerance** — the spout catch area is only 14 mm wide.
3. **Fill detection** — steam confuses the depth camera near the rim.

## Tasks

1. [ ] Classify cup material before gripping
2. [ ] Place cup within ±5 mm of spout center
3. [ ] Detect 90% fill level via weight on the tray scale
4. [ ] Retract cup along a splash-free trajectory

## Design

### Grip force table

| Material | Grip force | Approach speed | Failure mode |
|---|---|---|---|
| Paper | 3.5 N | slow | crush |
| Ceramic | 9.0 N | medium | slip |
| Glass | 7.0 N | slow | chip |
| Steel travel mug | 12.0 N | fast | none |

### Fill detection state machine

```text
EMPTY --(weight > 20g)--> FILLING
FILLING --(delta < 1g/s)--> SETTLING
SETTLING --(weight stable 2s)--> FULL
FULL --(cup removed)--> EMPTY
```

### Retraction trajectory

```python
def retract(arm, cup_pose):
    # lift straight up before any lateral motion to clear the spout
    arm.move_rel(z=+30, speed=SLOW)
    arm.move_rel(x=-120, speed=MEDIUM)
    return arm.settle(max_tilt_deg=3)
```

## Risks

- Steam condensation on the wrist camera lens after ~5 consecutive brews.
- The tray scale drifts 2 g per degree Celsius; needs recalibration hook.
- Espresso cups are below the classifier's training distribution.

## Acceptance

- 20 consecutive fills with zero spills across all four cup materials
- Fill level within ±8% of target across the run
- End-to-end cup cycle under 25 seconds
